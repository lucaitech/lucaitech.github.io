IRP Maturity Gap Closure Project

A post‑acquisition initiative focused on aligning two Incident Response Plans (IRPs), identifying maturity gaps, and elevating the combined organization’s response posture to industry best‑practice standards.

Project Overview

After our organization acquired another financial‑sector entity, I was tasked with evaluating the differences between both Incident Response Plans (IRPs). The goal was to identify maturity gaps, benchmark both plans against industry best practices, and propose enhancements that would unify and strengthen the combined IRP.

This work required strict confidentiality, careful redaction, and a methodical approach to ensure no sensitive information was exposed while still improving the organization’s operational readiness and regulatory alignment.

Objectives

  • Compare both IRPs at a structural and procedural level
  • Identify gaps in maturity, clarity, and best‑practice alignment
  • Recommend enhancements to strengthen response posture
  • Ensure all improvements remained compliant and non‑identifying

Key Findings & Improvements Proposed

1. RACI Matrix Integration

The acquired entity used a structured RACI model to clarify responsibilities across all response phases. Our IRP referenced roles but lacked a formalized matrix. I recommended implementing a high‑level, role‑based RACI to improve accountability and cross‑team coordination.

2. Incident Severity Classification

The acquired IRP included a clear severity scale defining low, medium, high, and critical incidents. Our IRP stated severity would be defined but did not include a visual or descriptive matrix. I proposed adding a severity chart to reduce ambiguity and standardize escalation.

3. Notification Flow & Escalation Triggers

The acquired IRP documented escalation logic, communication timelines, and decision points. I recommended adding a notification flow diagram to our IRP outlining when escalation occurs, who is notified, and expected communication timelines.

4. Testing, Training & Exercises (TT&E)

Both organizations conducted tabletop exercises and simulations, but only the acquired entity documented them. I recommended adding TT&E details, including frequency and exercise types, to demonstrate operational maturity.

5. Compliance Measurement & Reporting

The acquired IRP outlined compliance ownership and reporting cadence. I recommended adding a compliance governance section describing how adherence is tracked and how continuous improvement is measured.

6. Emergency Contacts & Cyber Insurance

The acquired IRP included a role‑based emergency contact section and cyber insurance considerations. I recommended adding a non‑sensitive, role‑based contact section to our IRP to improve clarity during high‑pressure events.

7. Definitions & Lessons Learned Process

The acquired IRP included definitions and step‑by‑step breakdowns for concepts like “incident closure” and “post‑incident analysis.” I recommended adding a definitions appendix and outlining our lessons‑learned workflow, including:

  • Post‑incident review
  • Root cause analysis
  • Response evaluation
  • Documentation
  • Policy updates
  • Knowledge sharing
  • Training & testing

Outcome

The final deliverable was a structured set of recommendations that significantly strengthened the organization’s IRP. The enhancements improved clarity, accountability, communication flow, compliance posture, and operational readiness. This project demonstrated my ability to perform IRP maturity assessments, benchmark against best practices, and drive cross‑team alignment during post‑acquisition integration — all while maintaining strict confidentiality in a regulated financial environment.
