My Journey Into Cybersecurity

I got into tech the same way a lot of 90s kids did — through gaming. When your machine couldn’t run the newest DOOM or Warcraft, you had two choices: give up, or hop on your bike and ride to the only computer shop in town for a stick of RAM that had to be the magic fix. That was me. One upgrade turned into another, and before long I was swapping hard drives, power supplies, and eventually building my first PC from scratch with a friend coaching me through the nerve‑wracking process. That moment stuck with me.

Even though I didn’t start my professional life in IT, tech never really left my orbit. It followed me into audio production, video editing, and graphic design — and it definitely made me the unofficial “family tech support” guy. It was always there, waiting for me to take it seriously.

When COVID hit in 2019 and entire industries collapsed, I had to make a choice. I went back to the one thing I knew I could rely on: tech. I landed a Tier 1 help desk role, and years of AV work in high‑pressure hotel environments had already sharpened my customer service instincts. Over the next few years, I grew into a strong Level 1 and Level 2 technician.

By 2023, I knew I wanted more. Cybersecurity wasn’t a random pivot — it was a natural progression. I was already helping with anti‑phishing efforts, managing access in Varonis, offboarding accounts in AD, and honestly, I’ve always loved criminology and cybercrime. It just clicked.

I earned my Google Cybersecurity Certificate, asked a lot of questions, and had the support of an open‑minded Security Operations Manager who gave me room to grow. That’s where I developed hands‑on experience with CrowdStrike, Varonis, Rapid7, Splunk, SolarWinds, Wiz, change management, and risk and governance work.

Today, I’m continuing that journey. I’m studying for the CISA and leaning deeper into Governance, Risk, and Compliance — a space where I see my future and where I’m ready to put everything I’ve got.
