Phishing Reduction Initiative

This project highlights my hands-on work responding to phishing reports, educating users, and improving overall security awareness. While I did not run formal phishing campaigns, I played a key role in reducing successful phishing attempts by investigating suspicious emails, guiding employees, and reinforcing safe behavior. All examples below are fictionalized but accurately reflect the workflows I performed.

1. Investigating Reported Phishing Emails

Employees frequently forwarded suspicious emails for review. My job was to quickly determine whether the message was malicious, benign, or part of a known phishing pattern.

  • Inspecting sender domains and reply-to mismatches
  • Checking embedded URLs for redirects or spoofed login pages
  • Reviewing attachments for macros or unusual file types
  • Comparing messages to known phishing templates
  • Checking threat intel feeds for related campaigns
Example Investigation (Fictional):

Subject: “Urgent: Payroll Update Required”
Indicators: Suspicious domain, mismatched branding, fake HR signature
Action: Blocked sender, warned user, added domain to email filter
Result: Prevented further delivery to additional employees

2. User Coaching & Awareness

A major part of reducing phishing risk came from one-on-one coaching. When users reported suspicious emails—or fell for one—I helped them understand what happened and how to avoid similar threats in the future.

  • Explaining red flags (urgent language, mismatched domains, fake signatures)
  • Showing users how to preview links safely
  • Helping employees verify unexpected requests
  • Encouraging use of the “Report Phishing” button
  • Reinforcing that reporting is always better than ignoring
Example User Coaching (Fictional):

User: kthomas@company.com
Issue: Clicked a suspicious link but did not enter credentials
Action: Guided user through password reset + explained indicators
Result: User became a consistent reporter of suspicious emails

3. Reducing Repeat Offenders

Some employees struggled more than others with identifying phishing attempts. I worked with these individuals directly to build confidence and reduce risky behavior.

  • Reviewing past phishing attempts with the user
  • Explaining why they were targeted
  • Helping them recognize patterns in malicious emails
  • Ensuring MFA and password hygiene were in place

4. Strengthening Organizational Awareness

While I didn’t run formal phishing campaigns, my work contributed to a noticeable improvement in user behavior. Employees became more proactive in reporting suspicious messages, and fewer incidents required escalation.

Impact (Fictionalized but realistic):

- Increase in phishing reports from employees
- Decrease in users clicking suspicious links
- Faster identification of malicious senders
- Fewer escalations to security leadership

5. Final Outcome

This project demonstrates my ability to investigate phishing attempts, educate users, and strengthen organizational awareness. By combining technical analysis with clear communication, I helped reduce phishing-related risks and improve overall security posture.
