Vulnerability Management Dashboard

This project demonstrates how I interpret vulnerability scan data, prioritize remediation, and communicate risk to stakeholders. The examples below use fictionalized data modeled after real-world Rapid7/Nessus reporting formats.

1. Understanding the Scan Data

A typical vulnerability scan includes severity ratings, exploitability, affected assets, and remediation steps. My first step is always to categorize findings into meaningful groups:

  • Critical vulnerabilities with known exploits
  • High-risk vulnerabilities on critical assets
  • Medium/Low findings that can be batched
  • Configuration weaknesses (TLS, SMB, outdated protocols)
Sample Scan Summary (Fictional):

Critical: 12
High: 47
Medium: 103
Low: 88
Total Assets Scanned: 214

2. Prioritization Framework

I prioritize vulnerabilities using a combination of:

  • CVSS score
  • Exploit availability (Metasploit, CISA KEV list)
  • Asset criticality (servers, domain controllers, finance systems)
  • Exposure (internet-facing vs internal)
  • Business impact
Example Prioritization:
  • Critical + Known Exploit + Internet-Facing → Immediate remediation
  • High + Privileged Asset → 7-day SLA
  • Medium + Workstation → Batch into monthly patch cycle

4. Communication & Remediation Workflow

My communication around vulnerabilities was done on an as‑needed basis rather than recurring weekly summaries. When critical or high‑risk issues were identified, I coordinated directly with the appropriate engineering or system owners to ensure the findings were understood and remediation was scheduled. Once fixes were applied, I validated them through rescans or follow‑up checks.

  • Notifying system owners when critical/high vulnerabilities were discovered
  • Providing context on exploitability and business impact
  • Coordinating remediation timelines based on urgency
  • Validating fixes through rescans or verification steps
  • Escalating only when necessary (e.g., overdue critical items)

5. Final Outcome

By implementing structured prioritization and targeted communication, I helped reduce vulnerability backlogs, improve patch compliance, and strengthen overall security posture.
